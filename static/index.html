Help & Guidelines

Learn how to use the system effectively and understand results

📷 Image Guidelines

  • Use good lighting — avoid shadows on the sample
  • Fill the frame with the food sample for best accuracy
  • Use a white or neutral background where possible
  • Ensure the image is sharp and in focus
  • For milk/honey, use a transparent glass or bowl
  • For powders, spread evenly on a flat white surface

🟡 Turmeric & Chilli

  • Pure Turmeric: Deep vibrant yellow-orange, uniform fine texture
  • Chalk adulteration: Pale yellow with white/cream patches
  • Pure Chilli: Vibrant red with consistent fine powder texture
  • Brick adulteration: Dull brownish-red with coarse dark clusters

🥛 Milk & Honey

  • Pure Milk: Creamy white, opaque with uniform appearance
  • Water adulteration: Translucent, bluish tinge, less opaque
  • Pure Honey: Rich amber with characteristic thick flow lines
  • Syrup adulteration: Lighter amber, whitish dilution patches

🍚 Sugar & Coriander

  • Pure Sugar: Bright white, sparkling crystal texture
  • Chalk adulteration: Off-white, dull with irregular clumps
  • Pure Coriander: Medium green-brown, fine uniform texture
  • Sawdust adulteration: Darker, coarser with fibrous patches

📊 Understanding Results

  • PURE ✅: No adulterants detected in the sample
  • ADULTERATED ⚠️: Foreign substance detected — report to authorities
  • INVALID ❌: Not a recognizable food sample image
  • Confidence: Higher = more certain. Above 85% is highly reliable
  • For borderline results (60-85%), test multiple samples

🔬 Detection Technology

  • Analyzes color distribution across all image pixels
  • Measures texture gradients for surface granularity
  • Detects light refraction patterns in liquid samples
  • Compares RGB channel ratios against pure baselines
  • Uses Random Forest ensemble (400 decision trees)
  • Trained on 5,200 synthetic images across 13 classes
